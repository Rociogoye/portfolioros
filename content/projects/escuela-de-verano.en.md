## The project

Landing page created for the **3rd Summer School for labor guidance professionals** by Orientadora de Orientadores. The page presents a five-day training experience with 19 live sessions, guest professionals, and different topics related to employability and guidance.

The challenge was to turn a large amount of information into a clear, persuasive, and easy-to-browse landing page without losing key details or overwhelming the user. The page had to support acquisition and guide the booking decision.

[Visit the 2026 Summer School](https://orientadoradeorientadores.com/formacion/escuela-verano-orientacion-laboral/)

## Context and problem

- Client: Orientadora de Orientadores.
- Need: present an intensive training with lots of content and convert interest into bookings.
- Problem: too much potentially scattered information for a commercial landing page.

## My role

- Built the landing in WordPress with Elementor.
- Organized the content architecture and message hierarchy.
- Decided what needed to appear first to reduce friction.
- Adapted the page for desktop and mobile to preserve readability and conversion.

## Working process

1. Reviewed the brief and training proposal.
2. Grouped the content into blocks: proposition, benefits, program, team, FAQs, and CTA.
3. Defined the reading order to guide the purchase decision.
4. Implemented the page visually in WordPress and Elementor.
5. Applied responsive adjustments and final QA before publishing.

## Coordination and decisions

- Worked within the visual identity already defined by the brand.
- Prioritized linear navigation and quick mobile reading.
- Simplified blocks to avoid a page that felt long but heavy.
- Reinforced calls to action where the decision was most likely to happen.

## Result

- Published and live landing page.
- 19 sessions presented in an orderly way.
- Program, speakers, and booking terms visible in one experience.
- A useful example of real delivery with client, content, and UX.

## Tools

The project was built with **WordPress and Elementor**, applying responsive design adjustments and visual content organization.
